## üìä Evaluation Framework Detail

### Criterion 1: Power and Energy Infrastructure ‚ö°

**Why This Matters**: Grid connection is the primary constraint in UK/Europe due to widespread congestion, long lead times, and decarbonisation mandates.

**Sub-Criteria** (6):
1. **Grid connection availability and lead time**
   - Assessment of DNO queue position and connection timeline
   - Critical: Connection delays of 3-7 years are common

2. **Proximity to primary or grid substation**
   - Distance significantly impacts connection cost
   - Closer proximity = lower risk and cost

3. **Available import capacity and reinforcement risk**
   - Existing headroom on local network
   - Reinforcement risk assessment

4. **Electricity reliability and outage history**
   - Historical CML and CI metrics
   - Network resilience characteristics

5. **Access to renewable energy or green tariffs**
   - On-site renewable potential
   - PPA availability

6. **Ability to support backup generation and energy storage**
   - Planning permission for standby generation
   - Space for battery systems

**Scoring Guidance**:
- **5 (Excellent)**: <12 months connection, <500m to substation, 5MW+ available
- **3 (Average)**: 18-36 months, 1-3km distance, some reinforcement needed
- **1 (Very Poor)**: >5 years, >5km, major reinforcement required

---

### Criterion 2: Network and Latency üåê

**Why This Matters**: Edge computing requires low-latency connectivity to end users and carrier diversity for resilience.

**Sub-Criteria** (5):
1. **Fibre density and route diversity**
   - Number of diverse fibre routes
   - Multiple physical paths

2. **Number of available carriers**
   - Competitive carrier market
   - Multiple tier-1 options

3. **Latency to target population or enterprise clusters**
   - <5ms to primary user base
   - Geographic positioning

4. **Proximity to mobile core or MEC nodes**
   - Mobile operator infrastructure
   - 5G edge positioning

5. **Regional and international connectivity resilience**
   - International gateway access
   - Pan-European connectivity

**Scoring Guidance**:
- **5 (Excellent)**: 5+ carriers, <2ms latency, multiple diverse routes
- **3 (Average)**: 2-3 carriers, 3-5ms latency, limited diversity
- **1 (Very Poor)**: Single carrier, >10ms, no diversity

---

### Criterion 3: Property and Site Characteristics üè¢

**Why This Matters**: Physical site suitability must be assessed independently from infrastructure‚Äîproperty constraints can derail projects regardless of power availability.

**Sub-Criteria** (6):
1. **Land or building availability**
   - Ownership clarity
   - Acquisition timeline

2. **Site size and shape suitability**
   - Adequate footprint for phased growth
   - Efficient layout potential

3. **Planning use class compatibility**
   - Current use class (B2/B8/Sui Generis)
   - Change of use requirements

4. **Structural loading and floor to ceiling height**
   - For existing buildings: floor loading capacity
   - Minimum 4m ceiling height preferred

5. **Setback, access and construction logistics**
   - HGV access
   - Construction compound space

6. **Security and boundary conditions**
   - Perimeter security potential
   - Site isolation/separation

**Scoring Guidance**:
- **5 (Excellent)**: Shovel-ready, optimal size, suitable use class, excellent access
- **3 (Average)**: Some adaptation needed, adequate size, achievable use class
- **1 (Very Poor)**: Major constraints, insufficient size, complex planning

---

### Criterion 4: Planning Compliance and Regulatory üìã

**Why This Matters**: UK planning regimes vary significantly by local authority; European sites face additional complexity from varying national regulations.

**Sub-Criteria** (5):
1. **Planning permission likelihood**
   - Local plan policy alignment
   - Precedent for data centres

2. **Local authority support and policy alignment**
   - Council attitude towards DCs
   - Economic development priority

3. **Environmental and noise compliance**
   - Noise impact on residential areas
   - Ecological constraints

4. **Data protection and sovereignty constraints**
   - GDPR compliance positioning
   - Government/critical infrastructure location requirements

5. **Permitting timeline and approval risk**
   - Expected approval duration
   - Appeal risk assessment

**Scoring Guidance**:
- **5 (Excellent)**: Policy support, <6 months planning, low objection risk
- **3 (Average)**: Neutral policy, 6-12 months, moderate risk
- **1 (Very Poor)**: Policy conflict, >18 months, high refusal risk

---

### Criterion 5: Cost and Commercial Viability üí∞

**Why This Matters**: Long-term financial sustainability requires holistic TCO assessment beyond just acquisition cost.

**Sub-Criteria** (6):
1. **Land or building acquisition cost**
   - ¬£/sqm or ¬£/acre
   - Market comparison

2. **Power connection and reinforcement cost**
   - DNO connection quote
   - Reinforcement contribution

3. **Electricity price structure and volatility**
   - Available tariffs
   - Price risk hedging

4. **Network access cost**
   - Dark fibre or wavelength costs
   - Build cost if required

5. **Local tax environment and incentives**
   - Business rates
   - Available grants/incentives

6. **Estimated total cost of ownership**
   - 10-year TCO model
   - All-in cost per kW

**Scoring Guidance**:
- **5 (Excellent)**: Below-market acquisition, minimal connection cost, favorable TCO
- **3 (Average)**: Market-rate pricing, moderate connection cost, competitive TCO
- **1 (Very Poor)**: Premium pricing, high connection cost, uncompetitive TCO

---

### Criterion 6: Sustainability and ESG Alignment üå±

**Why This Matters**: ESG requirements are mandatory for institutional investors and increasingly required for planning consent in Europe.

**Sub-Criteria** (5):
1. **Grid carbon intensity**
   - Regional grid carbon content (gCO2/kWh)
   - Decarbonisation trajectory

2. **Heat reuse potential**
   - Nearby heat network or demand
   - District heating connection potential

3. **Water usage and cooling efficiency**
   - Water-stressed area classification
   - Air cooling feasibility

4. **Alignment with local sustainability targets**
   - Local carbon neutral targets
   - Green infrastructure policies

5. **ESG reporting and certification readiness**
   - Ability to achieve BREEAM/LEED
   - EU Taxonomy alignment

**Scoring Guidance**:
- **5 (Excellent)**: Low carbon grid, heat reuse opportunity, strong ESG credentials
- **3 (Average)**: Average carbon, no heat reuse, basic ESG compliance
- **1 (Very Poor)**: High carbon, water constraints, weak ESG positioning

### Storage

- **Local Storage**: Browser localStorage API
- **Keys**: 
  - `dc_matrix_sites`: Array of site objects
  - `dc_matrix_weights`: Criteria weight configuration
- **Persistence**: Automatic on every change
- **Data Portability**: CSV export available

### Calculation Logic

```
Criterion Score = Average(Sub-Criteria Scores)

Total Score = Œ£(Criterion Score √ó Criterion Weight) / Œ£(Weights)
```

Example:
```
Power Score = (5 + 4 + 3 + 4 + 5 + 4) / 6 = 4.17
Weighted Power = 4.17 √ó 25% = 1.04

Total = (Power Weighted + Network Weighted + ... + Sustainability Weighted)
```
